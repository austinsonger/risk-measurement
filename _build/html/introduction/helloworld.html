Hello World!¶

Let’s quickly discuss some example outputs of this method.

One principle of this method is that it should be portable to any form of risk. To get a hang of it, it helps to understand how it is applicable to many industries.

Inline are links that lead into the use of estimation techniques into the industry itself.

Tip

Your risks are unique. These examples might not apply to your risk. That is ok!

With this method, we structure risks as a scenario, which will look similar to the examples below. Then, we forecast the values associated with them. If these decisions are important, in increase the rigor involved with the process. `

An example in Cyber-Security¶

A 16 person panel forecasted the likelihood of the following scenario. [0]

Scenario

Will a “Critical” Chromium exploit be discovered “in the wild” in September 2018?

Yes with a likelihood of 1.64%.

An example in Nuclear¶

Nuclear is concerned with combinations of events that may result in core damage or other outcomes. Expert elicitation techniques in Nuclear risk measurement are common. [1, 2]

Scenario

Will a pump failure result in core damage within the next year?

Yes with a likelihood of 0.001%

An example in Environmental Safety¶

Environmental impact organizations use the probabilistic risk assessment. [3, 4]

Scenario

How much will next years mortality rate due to Fine Particles (PM2.5) decrease if we pass X regulation?

Interval estimate: Reduction of .001 -.05% with 95% confidence.

An example in Meteorology¶

The United States spends billions on weather forecasting and its associated infrastructure. [5]

Scenario

Will the east coast hurricane make landfall near our city before we can evacuate?

Yes with a likelihood of 50%.

An example in Intelligence Analysis¶

All forms of intelligence gathering ultimately desire to inform decision making. [6, 7]

Scenario

Does the image taken by our satellite depict an adversary military aircraft?

Yes with a likelihood of 70%.
